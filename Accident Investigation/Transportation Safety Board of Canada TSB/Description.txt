The Transportation Safety Board of Canada (TSB) is an independent agency created by an act of Canada's Parliament in 1990 to advance safety by investigating occurrences in the air, marine, pipeline and rail modes of transportation. To ensure its independence, the TSB is separate from other government departments and reports to Canada’s Parliament through the president of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada. The legislation that created the TSB was the Canadian Transportation Investigation and Safety Board Act. The Board is comprised of five members, including the current chairperson, Kathy Fox.
