The following is a list of generally accepted considerations and techniques for ditching that should be applicable to virtually any fixed wing aircraft type. In the time available: Once the aircraft has come to a complete stop, initiate the evacuation. The expected attitude of the aircraft in the water is almost always "nose up" so that the rear doors will not be usable for evacuation. Ensure all available survival gear is taken from the aircraft. Whenever possible, link all of the rafts together to maximize the visual target for the search parties.
