This article considers some of the airmanship aspects , which are applicable generically to all aircraft and situations, relating to an encounter with radioactive material in flight or while on the ground. It does not provide guidance on decontamination or the handling of radioactive cargo. References sometimes use the term "radioactive cloud" is sometimes made in references. In this context, the term "cloud" means a contaminated volume of air and is not necessarily associated with moisture. As with ash clouds (see Volcanic Ash and Pyrocumulus), there are no physical or visible boundaries to the contaminated volumes, only arbitrary borderlines denoting when the contamination is deemed to be significant.
