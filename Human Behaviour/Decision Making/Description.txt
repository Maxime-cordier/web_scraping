Decision-making generally has four components: Defining the problem. Considering the options. Selecting and implementing an option. Reviewing the outcome.. Decision making is influenced by: Situational Awareness - a combination of availability of information and the ability of the decision maker to interpret the information (skills and professional knowledge). Experience - familiarity with similar, perhaps routine, situations.. Social - social pressure, national and institutional culture.. Situational limitations - Physiological & psychological pressures (stress, fatigue, hypoxia etc), time pressure.
