Poor coordination (or lack thereof) may result in: Loss of Separation at Sector Boundaries;. Airspace Infringement, e.g. an aircraft entering a prohibited or danger area shortly after crossing the boundary;. Loss of Communication, e.g. in case of failure to inform the neighbouring unit of a frequency change or failure to coordinate the proper frequency;. Increased workload, especially for the accepting sector. A situation that was supposed to be coordinated but was not is often discovered after some time and has the potential to ruin the controller's plan. A new plan needs to be made in such situation, with less than the usual time available.. Runway Incursion which could also be followed by a Missed Approach and subsequent need to rearrange all the traffic..
