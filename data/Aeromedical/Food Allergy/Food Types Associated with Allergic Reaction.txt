Many foods can trigger an allergic reaction; this may occur upon the first known ingestion. Common triggering foods vary around the world depending on the diet. Ingestion of, or exposure to, the following foods may cause a reaction: Peanuts,. Soy,. Wheat,. Tree nuts (pecans, pistachios, pine nuts, and walnuts),. Shellfish,. Fish,. Milk (dairy products generally from cows, goats, or sheep),. Seeds (sesame and poppy) and oils derived from them,. Rice,. Eggs (the white rather than the yolk), and. Food additives (including colouring)..
