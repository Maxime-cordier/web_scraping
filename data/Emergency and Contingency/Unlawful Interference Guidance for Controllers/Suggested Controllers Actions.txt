Best practice embedded in the ASSIST principle could be followed: (A - Acknowledge; S - Separate, S - Silence; I - Inform, S - Support, T - Time) A - exercise utmost caution when acknowledging the observed crew’s actions, do NOT ask for the crews’ intentions S - separate the aircraft from other traffic, prioritise it for landing (allow long final if requested), keep the active runway clear of departures, arrivals and vehicles S - silence the non-urgent calls (as required) and use separate frequency where possible I - inform the airport emergency services and all concerned parties according to the local procedures S - support the flight being hijacked with any information requested and deemed necessary (e.g. type of approach, runway length and aerodrome details, etc.) T - provide time for the crew to assess the situation, don’t press with non-urgent matters
