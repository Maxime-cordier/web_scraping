The Ganzavia GAK-22 Dino was an unusual light utility aircraft built in Hungary in the early 1990s. In configuration, it was a biplane with cantilever wings and a very pronounced negative stagger, making it almost a tandem wing design. The pilot and a single passenger sat side by side under an expansive bubble canopy, and it had fixed tricycle undercarriage. The fuselage was of welded steel tube construction, and the wings of duralumin, with the whole aircraft skinned in fabric, other than the forward fuselage which had aluminium skin. Wing span (metric): 7.60 m. Length (metric): 6.20 m. Height (metric): 2.80 m. Powerplant: 1 x Lycoming O-235-H2C four-cylinder horizontally opposed aircooled piston engine, 86 kW (115 hp).
