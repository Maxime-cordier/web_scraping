The de Havilland Canada DHC-2 Beaver is a single engined, high wing, propeller-driven, Short Take-Off and Landing (STOL) aircraft developed by de Havilland Canada, primarily known as a bush plane. It is used for cargo and passenger hauling, aerial application (crop dusting and aerial topdressing), and has been widely adopted by armed forces as a utility aircraft. Wing span (metric): 14.63 m. Length (metric): 9.22 m. Height (metric): 2.74 m. Powerplant: 1 x Pratt & Whitney R-985 Wasp Jr. radial engine, 450 hp (336 kW).. Engine Model(s): Pratt & Whitney R-985. 1 x Pratt & Whitney R-985 Wasp Jr. radial engine, 450 hp (336 kW).
