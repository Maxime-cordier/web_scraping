The Mil Mi-38 is a helicopter designed by Mil. Originally intended as a replacement for the Mil Mi-8 and the Mi-17, it is being marketed in both military and civil versions. It flew for the first time on 22 December 2003. Wing span (metric): 21.10 m. Length (metric): 19.70 m. Height (metric): 5.13 m. Powerplant: 2 x Klimov TV7-117V or PW127TS turboshaft, 1,864 kW (2,500 shp) each.. Engine Model(s): Klimov TV7-117. 2 x Klimov TV7-117V or PW127TS turboshaft, 1,864 kW (2,500 shp) each.
