The Bombardier Challenger 600 series (600/601/604/605) is a family of business jets designed by Bill Lear and produced first by Canadair until that company was bought by Bombardier Aerospace in 1986. The Challenger was one of the first business jets designed with a supercritical wing. Wing span (metric): 19.60 m. Length (metric): 20.85 m. Height (metric): 6.30 m. Powerplant: 2 x General Electric CF34-3A. Engine Model(s): General Electric CF34. 2 x General Electric CF34-3A
