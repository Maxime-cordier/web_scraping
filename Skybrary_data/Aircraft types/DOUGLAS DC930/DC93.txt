Short to medium range airliner. In service since 1965. First short range airliner in new design with rear fuselage mounted engines, T-tail and swept wings. Built in these versions: 10 initial type (DOUGLAS DC-9-10). 30 stretched version with more powerful engines,. 20 (DOUGLAS DC-9-20) combines fuselage of the 10 with engines of the 30.. The DC-9-30 was converted as freighter and mil. type C-9. 94 aircraft of -10, 5 of -20 and 520 of -30 remain in service in 1999. The DC93 is member of the DC-9 family of aircraft. Wing span (metric): 28.47 m. Length (metric): 36.37 m. Height (metric): 8.38 m. Powerplant: 10: 2 x P&W JT8D-7 (2 x 54,5 kN) turbofans or 20/30: 2 x P&W JT8D-9 (64,5 kN) or 2 x P&W JT8D-15 (66,7 kN) turbofans.. Engine Model(s): Pratt & Whitney JT8D. 10: 2 x P&W JT8D-7 (2 x 54,5 kN) turbofans or 20/30: 2 x P&W JT8D-9 (64,5 kN) or 2 x P&W JT8D-15 (66,7 kN) turbofans.
