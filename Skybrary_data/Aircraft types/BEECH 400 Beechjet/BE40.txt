Light corporate jet. In Service since 1986. Beechcraft/Raytheon bought the MITSUBISHI MU-300 Diamond design and has developed it into the BEECH 400. Also built as military T-1A JAYHAWK trainer. The original BEECH 400 and 400A is now marketed by RAYTHEON, USA. Remanufactured variants were built by Nextant Aerospace since 2010. The P&W JT15D-5 engines replaced with Williams FJ44-3AP ones and the aircraft EFIS and TAWS were upgraded. The Hawker 400XPR modification, featuring new avionics, interior and winglets, is availabke since 2014. Wing span (metric): 13.30 m. Length (metric): 14.80 m. Height (metric): 4.20 m. Powerplant: 2 x 12.9 kN P&W JT15D-5 turbofans. Engine Model(s): Pratt & Whitney Canada JT15D. 2 x 12.9 kN P&W JT15D-5 turbofans
