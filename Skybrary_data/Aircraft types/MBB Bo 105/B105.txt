The MBB Bo 105 is a light, twin-engine, multi-purpose utility helicopter developed by Bölkow of Stuttgart, Germany. Production began under Messerschmitt-Bölkow-Blohm (MBB), which became a part of Eurocopter in 1991. Eurocopter continued to produce the Bo 105 until 2001, when it was replaced in the product line by the EC 135. Wing span (metric): 9.84 m. Length (metric): 11.86 m. Height (metric): 3.00 m. Powerplant: 2 × Allison 250-C20B turboshaft engines, 313 kW (420 shp) each. Engine Model(s): Allison 250. 2 × Allison 250-C20B turboshaft engines, 313 kW (420 shp) each
