The Bell 212 Twin Huey (also known as the Twin Two-Twelve) is a two-blade, twin-engine, medium helicopter that first flew in 1968. The 212 is marketed to civilian operators and has a fifteen-seat configuration, with one pilot and fourteen passengers.Based on the stretched fuselage Bell 205, the Bell 212 was originally developed for the Canadian Forces. Wing span (metric): 14.64 m. Length (metric): 17.43 m. Height (metric): 3.83 m. Powerplant: 1 x Pratt & Whitney Canada PT6T-3 or -3B turboshaft, 1,800 shp (1,342 kW).. Engine Model(s): Pratt & Whitney Canada PT6T. 1 x Pratt & Whitney Canada PT6T-3 or -3B turboshaft, 1,800 shp (1,342 kW).
