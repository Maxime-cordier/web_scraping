The Tupolev Tu-95 is a large, four-engine turboprop-powered strategic bomber and missile platform. First flown in 1952, the Tu-95 entered service with the Soviet Union in 1956 and is expected to serve the Russian Air Force until at least 2040. A naval development of the bomber is designated Tu-142. Wing span (metric): 51.10 m. Length (metric): 49.20 m. Height (metric): 12.12 m. Powerplant: 4 x Kuznetsov NK-12M turboprops, 11,000 kW each.. Engine Model(s): Kuznetsov NK-12. 4 x Kuznetsov NK-12M turboprops, 11,000 kW each.
