Protuberances and sproutings tend to form a whitish mass, with more or less vertical striations (grooves or channels in cloud formations, arranged parallel to the flow of air and therefore depicting the airflow).. Capillatus: Cumulonimbus characterized by the presence, mostly in its upper portion, of distinct cirriform parts of clearly fibrous or striated structure, frequently having the form of an anvil, a plume or a vast, more or less disorderly mass of hair. Cumulonimbus capillatus is usually accompanied by a shower or by a thunderstorm, often with squalls and sometimes with hail; it frequently produces very well-defined virga..
