SIGMET information is information issued by a meteorological watch office concerning the occurrence or expected occurrence of specified en-route weather phenomena which may affect the safety of aircraft operations. (ICAO Annex 3: Meteorology) A SIGMET gives a concise description of the phenomena in abbreviated plain language. The following are examples of weather phenomena that may be described in a SIGMET: Thunderstorms. Cyclones (Tropical Revolving Storms). Severe turbulence. Severe icing. Severe Mountain Waves. Dust or Sand Storms. Volcanic Ash.
