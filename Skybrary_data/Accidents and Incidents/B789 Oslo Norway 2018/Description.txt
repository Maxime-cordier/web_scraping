On 18 December 2018, a Boeing 787-9 (ET-AUP) being operated by Ethiopian Airlines on a scheduled international passenger flight from Oslo to Addis Ababa as ETH175 and due to make an intermediate stop at Stockholm was taxiing into a remote stand to be de-iced prior to departure when it collided, in normal night visibility, with a lighting mast. The lighting mast was damaged to the extent that it required replacement and the collision caused significant damage to the right outer wing of the aircraft but there was no resultant fuel leak. The accident scene once the 787 had stopped about 4 metres after the collision occurred. [Reproduced from the Official Report]
