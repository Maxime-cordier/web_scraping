The Team Supervisor did not adequately monitor the developing situation and did not notice that the level and complexity of current and anticipated traffic in the combined sector airspace had increased. As a result, no steps were taken to alleviate the sector controller’s workload.. Two formally stated Findings as to Risk were also identified as follows If controllers are unfamiliar with air traffic services equipment errors, they may focus their attention on error resolution to the detriment of monitoring and controlling the movement of aircraft, increasing the risk of air traffic conflicts.. If Team Supervisors do not adequately monitor controller workload in complex situations with high traffic levels, workload may exceed controller limits, increasing the risk of adverse consequences, such as a loss of separation.. Safety Action taken as a result of the event by ANSP NAV CANADA whilst the Investigation was in progress was noted to have included the following: The Checklist which must be completed after software update to the ATC system used has been reformatted to make it more likely that it will be properly completed.. An Operational Directive has been issued to relevant personnel at the Vancouver ACC requiring that the audible alert volume must never be set lower than setting 4.. An Operational Directive has been issued to require that before a team supervisor leaves the operational environment, another team supervisor or a senior controller must be tasked with continuing the ongoing oversight which they have been providing.. The Final Report of the Investigation was authorised for release on 18 March 2020 and it was officially released on 2 April 2020. No Safety Recommendations were made.
