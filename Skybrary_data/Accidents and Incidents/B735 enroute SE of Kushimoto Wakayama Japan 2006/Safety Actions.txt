The report contains a section with the actions that were taken: Actions taken by the company to prevent the recurrence. After this serious incident, the company issued an AMM Bulletin (Aircraft Maintenance Manual), which has higher priority than the TSI (Technical Service Information), to implement improvements including measures to ensure complete removal of water after water washing of the engines on aircraft of the same type as the aircraft involved in this serious incident. In addition, the company decided to carry out engine water washing at its own facilities. Actions taken by the aircraft manufacturing company to prevent the recurrence. The aircraft manufacturing companyâ€™s AMM issued before this serious incident had stipulated in one item of the procedure that the anti-ice switches and bleed switches should be in the OFF position as a preparation work performed prior to the water spraying of the water washing. However, after this serious incident, taking into account the importance of this work, the aircraft manufacturing company amended the AMM as of July 12, 2007 and stipulated each switch operation as two independent items.
