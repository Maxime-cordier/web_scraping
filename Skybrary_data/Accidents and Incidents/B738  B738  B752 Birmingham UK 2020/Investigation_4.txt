Airside Driving Permits will in future be of two types, one authorising access to the Manoeuvring (M) area and the other authorising access to the Runway (R), the latter having a one year validity.. A ‘Working Instruction’ has been issued which requires the load area of the particular vehicle involved to be kept “sterile and clear of materials and tooling” when in use and any exceptions to this requirement must be authorised by either the Engineering Supervisor or Senior Technicians using an email message and such authorisation obtained before any such exceptionally permitted use of the vehicle occurs.. The Final Report of the Investigation was published on 25 February 2021. No Safety Recommendations were made.
