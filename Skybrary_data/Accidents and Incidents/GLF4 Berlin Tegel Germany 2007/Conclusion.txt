Because the Gulfstream had stopped before crossing the north side Cat 2/3 holding point, it was considered that there had been “no immediate risk of an accident”. The Investigation determined that the Cause of the incident was the issuing of a clearance by ATC on the basis of inadequate situational awareness. It was also considered that there were two Contributing Factors were: the restricted view from the control tower to the aircraft. insufficient use of the airfield surface movement radar. The Final Report of the Investigation was published in September 2008. No Safety Recommendations were made.
