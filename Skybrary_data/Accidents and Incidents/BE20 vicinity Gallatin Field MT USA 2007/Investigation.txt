An Investigation was carried out by the National Transportation Safety Board (USA) (NTSB). It was eastablishd that the aircraft was equipped with TAWS Class 'B' equipment. Impact with terrain was found to have occurred approximately 13 nm north-northwest of the intended destination at a height of just over 700 feet above the elevation of the destination airport. The Probable Cause of the accident was determined as "the pilot's failure to maintain an adequate altitude and descent rate during a night visual approach" and it was additionally noted that "dark night conditions and mountainous terrain are factors in the accident".
