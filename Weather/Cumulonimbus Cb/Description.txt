The Cumulonimbus cloud (Cb) forms when three conditions are met: There must be a deep layer of unstable air.. The air must be warm and moist.. A trigger mechanism must cause the warm moist air to rise: Heating of the layer of air close to the surface. Rising ground forcing the air upwards (orographic uplift).
